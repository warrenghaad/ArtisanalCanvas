Key Notes on Structure
Unit: The core motif/shape/pattern (e.g., Spiral, Symmetry) is the through-line, showing its evolution.
Subunit: Historical/cultural context where the motif appears, highlighting shifts in complexity or application.
Geometric Concept & Complexity: Describes the motif's form and how it grows more sophisticated over time.
Subject(s): Interdisciplinary connections (Art, Math, Science, etc.).
Grade Cohorts: Activities build from recognition (K/1) to creation (2/3), analysis (4/5), and deep research/engineering (MS).
Implementation Guide
This curriculum map centers the geometric idea as the "hero," showing students how shapes and patterns transform across human history in response to cultural, environmental, and technological needs. Each unit explores a single motif through increasing complexity and diverse applications.

Teachers can move either vertically (following one motif through history) or horizontally (exploring a historical period through multiple motifs).
